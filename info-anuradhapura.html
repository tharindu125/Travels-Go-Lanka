Ancient City of Anuradhapura ‚Äì A Timeless Heritage Site

Anuradhapura, the first capital of Sri Lanka, is one of the most sacred and historically significant cities in the country. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, this ancient city is home to massive stupas, royal palaces, sacred temples, and centuries-old monasteries.

Why Visit Anuradhapura?

"Anuradhapura is a must-visit for history lovers, cultural explorers, and Buddhist pilgrims. Its awe-inspiring stupas, sacred relics, and ancient ruins make it one of the most fascinating places in Sri Lanka."

üìú History of Anuradhapura

Founded in the 4th century BCE, Anuradhapura flourished as the capital of Sri Lanka for over 1,300 years, serving as the center of Buddhism and Sinhalese civilization. The city was abandoned after invasions in the 10th century but remains a sacred pilgrimage site today.
